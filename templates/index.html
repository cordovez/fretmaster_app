Learning the Fretboard

This simple app was created to help you memorise the notes on the fretboard. It does this not by going down each string or each fret, but by grouping them around the keys as arranged by fourth intervals. Additionally, the arrangement of the notes for each key is based on one of the seven positions of the major scale

The diagram below illustrates that by placing your pinky finger in the any of the light-green notes on the bottom of the illustration, you have access to all 12 musical keys within the reach of your fingers, with only a shift of a single fret (from the light green to the dark green notes).

Following the light-green note arrows from the bottom, the keys are B, E, A, D, G, C, F. Then shifting to the dark-green notes, the sequence continues to B♭, E♭, A♭, D♭, G♭. I like to arrange them starting from B, as reading "BEAD" is easier to remember. Mnemonic: "BEAD [G]oes in [C]ircles, [F]lattened B♭E♭A♭D♭ [G]oes [♭]ack"

There are many benefits to learning the concept of this arrangement of the keys. Others explain it better than I could

The first logical step is to learn the shapes that each position of the major scale makes.
